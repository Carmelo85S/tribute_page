Alessandro Del Piero - Story of a legend

  • 1991 - Alessandro Del Piero's professional journey kicked off when he joined Padova in 1991, making his Serie B debut at the age of 16.
  • 1993 - Del Piero's transfer to Juventus in 1993 marked the beginning of an illustrious career with the Turin-based club.
  • 1995 / 1996 - Contributed significantly as Juventus clinched the Serie A title (Scudetto) and the Supercoppa Italiana.
  • 1996 - Juventus won the penalty shootout 4-2, with Del Piero contributing to the victory, securing the Champions League title for Juventus.
  • 1996 - Following their Champions League triumph, Juventus faced River Plate in the Intercontinental Cup, which pits the winners of the UEFA Champions League against the winners of the Copa Libertadores. The match ended 1-0 in favor of Juventus, with Del Piero scorer of the game.
  • 1997 - Played a key role in Juventus reaching the UEFA Champions League final, though they faced defeat against Borussia Dortmund.
  • 1997 / 1998 - Achieved the accolade of being the Serie A top scorer during the 1997-1998 season.
  • 1999 / 2000 - Del Piero played a pivotal role as Juventus clinched the Serie A championship during the 1999-2000 season.
  • 2001 / 2002 - Juventus secured another Serie A title, with Del Piero's contributions on the field being instrumental.
  • 2002 / 2003 - Assumed the captaincy of Juventus and played a pivotal role in securing another Serie A title, and to reach the champions league final against Milan.
  • 2004 / 2005 - Del Piero finished as one of the top scorers in Serie A, underlining his continued goal-scoring prowess.
  • 2006 - Del Piero played a crucial role in Italy's 2006 FIFA World Cup victory, contributing key performances and goals.
  • 2006 / 2007 - After Juventus was relegated to Serie B due to the Calciopoli scandal, Del Piero remained loyal, helping the team secure promotion back to Serie A.
  • 2010 / 2011 - Player of the year
  • 2011 / 2012 - In this season with Juventus, Del Piero celebrated another Serie A championship, adding to his illustrious list of domestic honors.
  • 2012 / 2014 - Play for Sidney FC.
  • 2014 - End his career in Indian Super League playing for Delhi Dynamos.
Alessandro del Piero's career is marked by excellence, sportsmanship, and a deep connection with the fans, making him an enduring figure in the world of football.

"Del Piero is synonymous with Juventus. He is a player who cannot be compared to others." - Marcello Lippi
